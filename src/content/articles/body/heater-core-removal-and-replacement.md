**Note from the Author**

This article will walk you through the removal of your heater core. The
factory manuals will tell you to remove the dashboard to gain access to the
heater core. Actually, the absence of the dashboard is not necessary. It would
make the heater core easier to reach, sure, but it would make the job much
harder overall! I did this job on a 1992 968 so this procedure should apply to
any late (1985.5-1991) 944 or 968.

## Tools

- Stubby Phillips head screwdriver
- Regular Phillips head screwdriver
- Small flat tip screwdriver
- Metric wrench (for the battery negative terminal)

## Removal

1. Disconnect the battery negative terminal.
2. Drain the coolant system.
3. Remove the glove box.
4. Disconnect the rubber hose for the thermometer (if equipped) and also remove the plastic air duct.

![Heater Core Removal and Replacement](/articles/heater-core-removal-and-replacement/968heatercore01.jpg)

5. These two plastic duct pieces must now be removed.

![Heater Core Removal and Replacement](/articles/heater-core-removal-and-replacement/968heatercore02.jpg)

6. The lower piece is secured with one screw. Undo it, and remove the piece.

![Heater Core Removal and Replacement](/articles/heater-core-removal-and-replacement/968heatercore03.jpg)

![Heater Core Removal and Replacement](/articles/heater-core-removal-and-replacement/968heatercore04.jpg)

7. Cut this zip-tie holding a wire to the upper duct piece.

![Heater Core Removal and Replacement](/articles/heater-core-removal-and-replacement/968heatercore05.jpg)

8. There are 3 screws holding the upper duct piece. Remove this screw pictured.

![Heater Core Removal and Replacement](/articles/heater-core-removal-and-replacement/968heatercore06.jpg)

9. Now remove these 2 screws.

![Heater Core Removal and Replacement](/articles/heater-core-removal-and-replacement/968heatercore07.jpg)

10. The yellow arrows show again the 2 upper screws for the upper duct piece. With all 3 screws out, pull and remove the piece.

![Heater Core Removal and Replacement](/articles/heater-core-removal-and-replacement/968heatercore08.jpg)

11. The piece in my hand. Note the shape, and note shape of where you just pulled it from, so you know how to reinstall it.

![Heater Core Removal and Replacement](/articles/heater-core-removal-and-replacement/968heatercore09.jpg)

12. Place a container under the heater core pipe connections to catch spilling coolant. I used tuperware. Use a screwdriver to pry off these 2 clips on the heater core pipes. Don't lose these clips!

![Heater Core Removal and Replacement](/articles/heater-core-removal-and-replacement/968heatercore10.jpg)

![Heater Core Removal and Replacement](/articles/heater-core-removal-and-replacement/968heatercore11.jpg)

13. Pull/hold these vacuum elements out of the way, along with anything else obstructing the foam-padded heater core.

![Heater Core Removal and Replacement](/articles/heater-core-removal-and-replacement/968heatercore12.jpg)

14. Start pulling out the core. Yea! The heater core is coming out, and you didn't even have to touch the dash!

![Heater Core Removal and Replacement](/articles/heater-core-removal-and-replacement/968heatercore13.jpg)

15. You will soon find the core won't simply slip out. It will run into the wire looms circled. It would
    seem the wiring was run AFTER the core was installed, because there just
    isn't enough clearance here. You need to undo the plastic strap here,
    which I have already done in this picture, and remove it completely. Then
    just push and pull on the wires while tugging out the core.

![Heater Core Removal and Replacement](/articles/heater-core-removal-and-replacement/968heatercore14.jpg)

16. Eventually the core will squeeze by and finally make it all the way out of its housing.

![Heater Core Removal and Replacement](/articles/heater-core-removal-and-replacement/968heatercore15.jpg)

17. The dotted circle shows the
    inevitable damage that will occur to your wire loom. As long as no
    insulation was broken, it's fine. The arrow above that shows the painted
    stud where that plastic strap was removed from.

![Heater Core Removal and Replacement](/articles/heater-core-removal-and-replacement/968heatercore16.jpg)

18. The heater core is now out.

![Heater Core Removal and Replacement](/articles/heater-core-removal-and-replacement/968heatercore17.jpg)

19. Installation is the reverse of removal.
