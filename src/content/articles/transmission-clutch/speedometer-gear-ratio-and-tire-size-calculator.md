| These calculations will not only help you determine your speedometer error, but will also help you determine your maximum speed in any gear for a given gear set. This can be particularly useful if you are considering changing to a transaxle with a different set of gears or to a different ring a pinion. It can also be used to determine how different wheel and tire size combinations will affect your speedometer. If you are trying to verify the accuracy of your speedometer, you should be confident that your tachometer is indicating correctly. If you doubt the accuracy of your tachometer, obtain a strobotach / stroboscope and check it or have a speedometer shop check it for you. You will also need to determine your gear sizes, final drive ratio, tire size, and wheel diameter to plug into the program. If you have an owner's manual, the gear sizes and final drive ratio will be listed there. For the tire and wheel size, simply look on the side of tires on your drive wheels (i.e. for a rear wheel drive car, look at the rear tires).
